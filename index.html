01 — The challenge

A global leader in circular technology — with almost no online presence.

Alchemy is a global leader in circular technology entering a critical growth phase. Initially briefed for media relations, we saw the opportunity to integrate social to strengthen authority and brand presence — positioning Alchemy as expert leaders, and lifting the reputation of the business and its spokespeople. For journalists, investors and partners doing due diligence, it was crucial the company appeared authoritative and active across digital channels.

— 01

No external marketing

Alchemy had done very little brand‑building before our engagement.

— 02

Almost zero online presence

A baseline of 5,248 LinkedIn followers and minimal owned content.

— 03

High‑stakes audience

OEMs, sustainability leads and international investors all watching closely.

02 — Objectives & budget

06 — How we cracked it

Three honest challenges. Three deliberate fixes.

01

Building engagement in a niche market

Engagement was modest at first as audiences assessed an unfamiliar brand. We used analytics to identify the best‑performing headlines, topics and posting times — turning sporadic traction into consistent, insight‑led gains.

02

Encouraging internal participation

Employee activity levels varied. Through coaching, curated asset packs and structured prompts, we turned hesitant staff into confident advocates — and senior leaders into independent posters, multiplying organic reach.

03

Synchronising PR & social

Shared calendars and real‑time feedback loops meant every press announcement or thought‑leadership article was immediately echoed on LinkedIn, sustaining momentum and visibility across the campaign.
